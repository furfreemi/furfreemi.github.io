Projects
Internships + Co-ops

Summer 2016: Emergency departments are fast-paced environment, and small, repeated actions such as logging into workstations or managing patient charts can end up consuming large amounts of a physician's time. This project further improved physician efficiency and patient experience by minimizing required workstation interactions.
Through mobile integration of physical Bluetooth buttons and a location context-based rules engine, this project allows physicians to pair selected button click combinations with customizable 'quick actions' to be performed throughout hospital logs and patient records. With a simple button press, physicians are able to complete actions such as opening a patient's chart or joining a patient's treatment team without having to touch a computer, allowing focus to remain on the patient.
Primary languages: Objective-C, JavaScript

Summer 2015: Emergency department visits can be terrifying. Throughout difficult-to-navigate hospitals, confusing workflows, long wait times, a constantly-rotating treatment team, and a huge amount of medical jargon combined with rushed tests and procedures, there are few environments more stressful for patients.
I had the opportunity to develop an iOS application for patient use throughout an emergency department visit, increasing patient understanding of emergency department processes and level of engagement in care. The app facilitates patient comfort through wait and result time estimation, customized explanations of procedures and prognoses, simplification of communication with staff, and a one-stop resource for test results, final diagnoses, and followup instructions.
Primary language: Objective-C

Spring + Summer 2014: Working on the infrastructure team, my project was a 'universal installer' automating a multi-computer system setup. My program automated the delivery and installation of customized ISO images from a console in order to initialize the specific operating system required by each computer, followed by the automated installation of user-selected system packages to specific computers in order to complete setup.
Primary languages: Java, Python
